STEEL

SUMMARY

Steel is an alloy primarily composed of iron and carbon, with trace amounts of other elements to enhance its properties. Known for its strength, durability, and versatility, steel is one of the most widely used materials in construction, manufacturing, and engineering industries worldwide.

Steel is essential in construction for beams, girders, and rebar. It is used in manufacturing vehicles, machinery, and appliances. Steel also plays a critical role in infrastructure projects like bridges, railways, and pipelines. Specialized steels, such as stainless steel, are used in medical equipment, food processing, and decorative applications.

Steel offers high tensile strength, ductility, and resistance to deformation. It can be tailored for specific applications through heat treatment and alloying. Stainless steel resists corrosion due to its chromium content, while high-strength steels are designed for load-bearing applications. Steel is also recyclable, making it a sustainable material.

Steel is highly sustainable, as it is one of the most recycled materials globally. The use of electric arc furnaces (EAFs) and renewable energy sources has reduced its carbon footprint.
