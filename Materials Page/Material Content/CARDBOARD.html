CARDBOARD

SUMMARY

Cardboard is a widely used material composed primarily of wood fibers or recycled paper, sometimes reinforced with adhesives. It is available in different types, such as corrugated cardboard, which features a fluted inner layer sandwiched between two flat liner boards for enhanced strength. Paperboard, another type, is lightweight and commonly used for packaging, while solid board is dense and suitable for heavier-duty applications. Cardboard typically has a smooth or slightly textured surface, often in brown or white, though it can be dyed or printed for various uses. Its flexibility and availability in various sizes make it ideal for a range of applications.

Cardboard is extensively used in packaging, shipping, and product storage due to its durability and lightweight nature. It is also employed in crafting, model-making, and 5 temporary structures in architecture and design. Additionally, it serves as a medium for creative projects like sculpture and prototyping.

Its properties include being lightweight, durable, and cost-effective. Corrugated cardboard offers excellent shock absorption and insulation, making it suitable for protecting fragile items. Cardboard is also easily cut, folded, and shaped, allowing for versatility in design and functionality. It is recyclable, biodegradable, and renewable, contributing to its environmental appeal.

From a sustainability perspective, cardboard is a greener option compared to plastics. It is recyclable and often made from recycled materials, reducing the demand for virgin resources. Its biodegradability ensures it breaks down naturally when disposed of properly, minimizing environmental impact. However, the production process can involve significant water and energy use, which underscores the importance of recycling to improve its overall sustainability profile.
