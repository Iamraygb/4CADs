COROPLAST

SUMMARY

Coroplast, a trademarked name for corrugated plastic, is a lightweight, durable, and versatile material made from polypropylene. It consists of two flat plastic sheets separated by a fluted core, similar in structure to corrugated cardboard. Coroplast is widely used for signage, packaging, and crafting applications due to its strength and water resistance.

Coroplast is primarily used for outdoor signage, including election signs, real estate boards, and advertisements. It is also employed in packaging for lightweight, reusable containers and in arts and crafts for projects requiring a sturdy yet flexible material. Other uses include temporary wall panels, pet enclosures, and industrial liners.

Coroplast is lightweight, weather-resistant, and resistant to most chemicals, making it ideal for outdoor and industrial applications. It is easy to cut, fold, and print on, allowing for creative customization. While durable, it is not suitable for high-heat applications as polypropylene has a low melting point.

Coroplast is made from polypropylene, a recyclable plastic, and can often be reused in multiple applications due to its durability. However, its environmental impact depends on proper recycling and disposal practices. Advances in biodegradable plastics and recycling technologies could further improve its sustainability.

Coroplast is produced by Coroplast LLC, a division of Inteplast Group, as well as other manufacturers like Primex Plastics and A&C Plastics. These companies offer coroplast sheets in various sizes, colors, and grades to cater to different industrial and commercial needs.
