TEXTILE

SUMMARY

Textiles are flexible materials made by weaving, knitting, crocheting, or bonding fibers, which can be natural (like cotton, wool, or silk) or synthetic (like polyester, nylon, or acrylic). They are commonly used in clothing, household items, and industrial applications.

Textiles are used in fashion (clothing, accessories), home furnishings (curtains, bedding, upholstery), industrial products (filters, insulation), and medical supplies (bandages, protective gear). They are also used in technical fabrics for sports, automotive, and aerospace sectors.

Textiles vary in properties depending on the fiber type but generally offer flexibility, breathability, insulation, and strength. Natural fibers tend to be more breathable and biodegradable, while synthetic fibers are often stronger, more durable, and water-resistant but may be less eco-friendly.

Textile sustainability depends on the source and production process. Natural fibers like cotton and wool are renewable but may require significant water and land use. Synthetic textiles, while durable, are petroleum-based and can be less biodegradable. Sustainable practices include using organic fibers, recycling textiles, and adopting eco-friendly production methods to reduce waste and energy consumption.
